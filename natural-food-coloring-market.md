<p><p>The natural food coloring market includes various types, each derived from different sources. Caramel color is produced by heating sugar, while capsanthin is extracted from red peppers. Turmeric provides a vibrant yellow hue, and carotenoids are sourced from plants, contributing orange and red colors. Annatto offers a reddish-yellow tint from the achiote seed, and carmine is derived from cochineal insects for red shades. Red beet adds a deep red color, spirulina introduces green-blue tones, and chlorophyll provides natural green coloring. Other categories may encompass lesser-known natural dyes.</p></p>
